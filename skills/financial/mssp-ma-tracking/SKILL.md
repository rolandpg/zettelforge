# MSSP M&A Tracking

## When to Use
Track and analyze MSSP consolidation trends, PE due diligence on acquisitions, and financial strategy for Patrick's PE advisory ambitions.
Use this skill to monitor deals, valuations, acquirer patterns, and maintain a deal tracker for strategic analysis.

## Procedure
1. Monitor MSSP and cybersecurity M&A activity:
   - Track deals via SEC filings, press releases, and financial news
   - Record valuations, acquirers, and thesis patterns
   - Monitor PE firms active in cybersecurity consolidation plays
   
2. Research relevant SEC filings:
   - Check 8-Ks for material events (acquisitions, divestitures)
   - Review 10-Q/10-K for MSSP segment performance
   - Track investor presentations and earnings calls from public cybersecurity companies
   
3. Maintain deal tracker file:
   - Update ~/cti-workspace/notes/mssp-ma-tracker.md with new transactions
   - Include: Date, Target, Acquirer, Deal Value, Multiple (if disclosed), Strategic Notes
   
4. Analyze Patrick's PE advisory positioning:
   - Identify gaps in current MSSP consolidation narrative
   - Refine the pitch: MSSP consolidation catalyst with CMMC expertise as differentiator
   - Map the network needed to build (PE firms, intermediaries, advisors)
   
5. Track macroeconomic indicators:
   - Interest rates, capital availability, sector sentiment for tech M&A
   - Credit markets, debt financing availability for leveraged deals
   
6. Research tax-advantaged strategies:
   - Relevant to W-2 plus advisory income profiles
   - 401(k), backdoor Roth, HSA, charitable giving strategies
   
7. Flag investment opportunities/risks:
   - Align with Patrick's financial goals and risk tolerance
   - Focus on MSSP targets with CMMC/DIB expertise as value drivers

## Examples
- Track a new MSSP acquisition: Add entry to mssp-ma-tracker.md with deal details
- Review SEC EDGAR for recent 8-K filings from cybersecurity companies
- Analyze PE firm portfolios: Which cybersecurity MSSPs do they own?
- Calculate implied multiples from disclosed deals: EV/EBITDA, Revenue multiples

## Gotchas
- Always verify deal values from primary sources (SEC filings, official press releases)
- Be cautious of rumors and unverified social media claims
- Distinguish between announced deals vs. completed transactions
- Watch for structured earnouts, rollover equity, and complex consideration
- Remember that PE valuations often differ from strategic acquirer valuations
