# ISO/IEC 27002:2022 — People Controls (6.1–6.8) & Physical Controls (7.1–7.14)

---

# SECTION 6: PEOPLE CONTROLS (8 controls)

## 6.1 Screening
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Identify | #Human_resource_security | #Governance_and_Ecosystem

**Purpose:** Ensure all personnel are eligible and suitable for their roles and remain so during employment.

**Key Points:**
- Perform screening for all personnel: full-time, part-time, temporary staff
- Include screening requirements in supplier service contracts
- Collect and handle candidate information per applicable legislation
- In some jurisdictions: legally required to inform candidates beforehand about screening activities

**Verification can include (where permitted):**
- Satisfactory references (business and personal)
- CV verification for completeness and accuracy
- Confirmation of academic and professional qualifications
- Independent identity verification (passport, etc.)
- Credit review or criminal records check for critical roles

- When hiring for IS role: ensure candidate has necessary competence and can be trusted
- Define criteria and limitations for verification reviews
- Where verification cannot be completed in time: mitigating controls (delayed onboarding, delayed asset deployment, reduced access onboarding, employment termination)
- Repeat verification periodically depending on criticality of role

---

## 6.2 Terms and Conditions of Employment
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Identify #Protect | #Human_resource_security | #Governance_and_Ecosystem

**Purpose:** Ensure personnel understand their IS responsibilities for their roles.

**Contractual agreements should state:**
- Personnel's and organization's IS responsibilities
- Compliance with IS policy and topic-specific policies

**Contracts should clarify:**
- Confidentiality/NDA agreements to sign prior to access being granted
- Legal responsibilities and rights (copyright, data protection)
- Responsibilities for classification and management of organizational information and assets
- Responsibilities for handling information from interested parties
- Actions if personnel disregard IS requirements

- Communicate IS roles and responsibilities to candidates during pre-employment
- Ensure personnel agree to IS-related terms and conditions
- Review terms when laws, regulations, or policies change
- Ensure responsibilities continue for defined period after end of employment where appropriate

---

## 6.3 Information Security Awareness, Education and Training
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Human_resource_security | #Governance_and_Ecosystem #Protection

**Purpose:** Ensure personnel and relevant interested parties are aware of and fulfil their IS responsibilities.

**Programme:**
- Establish IS awareness, education, and training programme aligned with IS policy
- Conduct periodically; initial awareness applies to new personnel and transfers
- Assess understanding at end of awareness/education/training activities

**Awareness:**
- Make personnel aware of responsibilities and how to discharge them
- Plan considering roles of personnel (internal and external)
- Schedule regularly; cover new personnel; repeat
- Build on lessons learned from IS incidents
- Use channels: campaigns, booklets, posters, newsletters, websites, information sessions, briefings, e-learning, emails

**Cover general aspects:**
- Management's commitment to IS
- Familiarity and compliance with applicable IS rules and obligations
- Personal accountability for actions and inactions
- Basic IS procedures (event reporting, password security)
- Contact points and resources for additional information

**Education and Training:**
- Identify, prepare, and implement training plans for technical teams
- Ensure technical teams have skills for configuring and maintaining the required security level
- Forms: lectures, self-studies, mentoring, on-the-job training, rotating staff, recruiting skilled people, hiring consultants
- Technical personnel should subscribe to newsletters, magazines, attend conferences

---

## 6.4 Disciplinary Process
**Attributes:** #Preventive #Corrective | #CIA | #Govern | #Human_resource_security | #Governance_and_Ecosystem

**Purpose:** Deter and appropriately deal with IS policy violations.

**Key Points:**
- Formalize and communicate disciplinary process
- Do not initiate without prior verification that a violation occurred
- Graduated response considering:
  - Nature (who, what, when, how) and gravity of breach and consequences
  - Whether intentional (malicious) or unintentional (accidental)
  - Whether first or repeated offence
  - Whether violator was properly trained
- Must comply with relevant legal, statutory, regulatory, contractual requirements
- Use as deterrent
- Deliberate violations can require immediate actions
- Protect identity of individuals subject to discipline where possible
- Reward excellent IS behavior where appropriate

---

## 6.5 Responsibilities After Termination or Change of Employment
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Human_resource_security | #Governance_and_Ecosystem

**Purpose:** Protect organization's interests as part of employment change or termination.

**Key Points:**
- Define which IS responsibilities remain valid after termination or change (confidentiality, IP, knowledge, responsibilities in NDAs)
- Responsibilities remaining valid should be in employment terms and conditions
- Manage changes of responsibility as termination of current + initiation of new
- Identify and transfer IS roles/responsibilities held by departing individual
- Establish process for communicating changes and operating procedures to personnel and relevant contacts
- Apply process to external personnel (suppliers) as well

---

## 6.6 Confidentiality or Non-Disclosure Agreements
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #Confidentiality | #Protect | #Human_resource_security #Legal_and_compliance | #Governance_and_Ecosystem

**Purpose:** Maintain confidentiality of information accessible by personnel or external parties.

**Key Points:**
- Identify, document, regularly review, and sign NDAs/confidentiality agreements reflecting organizational needs
- Agreements use legally enforceable terms to protect confidential information
- Applicable to interested parties and personnel

**Terms should consider:**
- Definition of information to be protected
- Expected duration including cases requiring indefinite confidentiality
- Required actions when agreement is terminated
- Responsibilities and actions to avoid unauthorized disclosure
- Ownership of information, trade secrets, and intellectual property
- Permitted use of confidential information and rights of signatory
- Right to audit and monitor activities involving confidential information (highly sensitive circumstances)
- Process for notification and reporting of unauthorized disclosure or leakage
- Terms for information to be returned or destroyed at termination
- Expected actions in case of non-compliance
- Take into consideration compliance with NDAs for the jurisdiction to which they apply
- Review requirements periodically and when changes occur

---

## 6.7 Remote Working
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Human_resource_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Ensure security of information when personnel are working remotely.

**Topic-specific policy should consider:**
- Existing or proposed physical security of remote working site and local environment
- Rules and security mechanisms for remote physical environment (lockable filing cabinets, secure transportation, clear desk, printing, disposal, event reporting)
- Expected physical remote working environments
- Communications security requirements including remote access needs and sensitivity
- Use of remote access (virtual desktop access)
- Threat of unauthorized access from other persons at remote site (family, friends)
- Threat in public places
- Use of home/public networks; requirements/restrictions on wireless network configuration
- Security measures: firewalls, malware protection
- Secure mechanisms for deploying and initializing systems remotely
- Secure mechanisms for authentication; vulnerability of single-factor authentication

**Guidelines should include:**
- Provision of suitable equipment and storage furniture where private equipment is not allowed
- Definition of permitted work; information classification that can be held; systems authorized to access
- Provision of training for remote workers and support personnel
- Provision of suitable communication equipment (screen locks, inactivity timers, location tracking, remote wipe)
- Physical security
- Rules on family and visitor access to equipment and information
- Hardware and software support and maintenance
- Provision of insurance
- Backup and business continuity procedures
- Audit and security monitoring
- Revocation of authority and access rights and return of equipment upon termination

---

## 6.8 Information Security Event Reporting
**Attributes:** #Detective | #CIA | #Detect | #Information_security_event_management | #Defence

**Purpose:** Support timely, consistent, and effective reporting of IS events by personnel.

**Key Points:**
- Provide mechanism for personnel to report observed or suspected IS events through appropriate channels in timely manner
- Make all personnel aware of responsibility to report IS events as quickly as possible
- Make personnel aware of reporting procedure and point of contact
- Ensure reporting mechanism is easy, accessible, and available

**Situations to consider for reporting:**
- Ineffective IS controls
- Breach of CIA expectations
- Human errors
- Non-compliance with IS policies or standards
- Breaches of physical security measures
- System changes not through change management
- Malfunctions or anomalous system behavior
- Access violations
- Vulnerabilities
- Suspected malware infection

- Advise personnel NOT to attempt to prove suspected vulnerabilities (can be interpreted as misuse and can cause damage or corrupt digital evidence)

# SECTION 7: PHYSICAL CONTROLS (14 controls)

## 7.1 Physical Security Perimeters
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Prevent unauthorized physical access, damage, and interference to organization's information and assets.

**Key Points:**
- Define security perimeters and siting/strength per IS requirements
- Physically sound perimeters (no gaps): solid construction of exterior roofs, walls, ceilings, flooring
- All external doors: protected against unauthorized access (bars, alarms, locks)
- Doors and windows locked when unattended
- External protection for windows, particularly at ground level
- Ventilation points must be considered
- Fire doors: alarm, monitor, and test; establish required fire resistance per standards; failsafe operation
- Additional barriers/perimeters may be necessary between areas with different security requirements
- Consider physical security measures that can be strengthened during increased threat situations

---

## 7.2 Physical Entry
**Attributes:** #Preventive #Detective | #CIA | #Protect #Detect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Ensure only authorized physical access occurs.

**Key Points:**
- Control access points (delivery areas, loading areas)
- Isolate entry points from information processing facilities where possible
- Restrict access to authorized personnel only
- Implement authentication mechanisms: keycards, PINs, biometrics as appropriate
- Visitor management: escorted at all times; access logs maintained
- Review and update access rights regularly
- Consider security monitoring at entry points (CCTV)
- Implement entry controls commensurate with classification of information in the area

---

## 7.3 Securing Offices, Rooms and Facilities
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Prevent unauthorized physical access, damage, and interference to offices, rooms, and facilities.

**Key Points:**
- Key facilities should be sited to avoid access by the public
- Buildings should be unobtrusive and give minimum indication of their purpose
- Directories and internal phone books identifying sensitive areas should not be accessible by the public
- Lock offices and rooms when vacated
- Separate sensitive processing areas and only allow access to authorized personnel
- Physical entry control for all sensitive processing areas
- Consider protecting against accidental damage to physical structures

---

## 7.4 Physical Security Monitoring
**Attributes:** #Detective | #CIA | #Detect | #Physical_security | #Defence

**Purpose:** Detect and deter unauthorized physical access.

**Key Points:**
- Implement continuous monitoring of areas as appropriate
- Consider CCTV, intruder detection systems, alarm systems
- Ensure monitoring covers entry and exit points, server rooms, sensitive areas
- Retain monitoring records for appropriate period
- Integrate monitoring with IS incident management process
- Review monitoring outputs regularly
- Ensure monitoring complies with applicable legislation (privacy, employee monitoring)

---

## 7.5 Protecting Against Physical and Environmental Threats
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Prevent or reduce consequences of events originating from physical and environmental threats.

**Key Points:**
- Identify and assess physical and environmental threats: natural (flood, fire, earthquake, storm); man-made (fire, theft, explosion)
- Implement controls proportionate to assessed threats
- Consider physical location relative to: flood plains; earthquake zones; storm corridors
- Fire: fire suppression systems, fire doors, smoke detectors, extinguishers
- Flood: flood barriers, elevated equipment, waterproof protection
- Consider redundant utilities in separate physical locations
- Define requirements for climate control, humidity, temperature

---

## 7.6 Working in Secure Areas
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Protect information processed in and through secure areas.

**Key Points:**
- Personnel should only be aware of the existence of a secure area on a need-to-know basis
- Avoid unsupervised working in secure areas for both safety and security
- Lock and regularly inspect vacant secure areas
- Do not allow photographic, video, audio, or other recording equipment in secure areas (unless authorized)
- Control and track personal electronic devices (mobile phones, laptops) brought into secure areas
- Define and communicate rules for working in secure areas

---

## 7.7 Clear Desk and Clear Screen
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #Confidentiality #Integrity | #Protect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Reduce risks of unauthorized access, loss, and damage to information and other assets.

**Key Points:**
- Implement clear desk policy: store sensitive/classified information in locked cabinets when not in use
- Store sensitive information in locked desk, cabinet, or other form of security furniture when not in use
- Computers and terminals should be logged off or locked when unattended
- Automatic locking mechanism should be configured
- Sensitive documents should not be left on printers or photocopiers
- Mail points, fax machines, telex machines, etc. should be protected
- Protect storage media containing sensitive information from unauthorized access

---

## 7.8 Equipment Siting and Protection
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Reduce risks from physical and environmental threats and from unauthorized access.

**Key Points:**
- Site equipment to minimize unnecessary access into work areas
- Site sensitive items where reduced public observation
- Implement controls to minimize risk of potential physical and environmental threats: theft, fire, explosives, smoke, water, dust, vibration, chemicals, interference with electrical supply, interference with communications
- Food and drink not permitted near information processing equipment
- Consider environmental conditions: temperature, humidity
- Monitor environmental conditions
- Protect special protection equipment (laptops) from risk to unattended equipment

---

## 7.9 Security of Assets Off-Premises
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Physical_security #Asset_management | #Protection

**Purpose:** Prevent loss, damage, theft, or compromise of off-premises assets and interruption to business operations.

**Key Points:**
- Equipment or information taken off site should be authorised; maintain log of authorisations
- Apply equivalent protection to off-premises equipment as on-premises
- Consider the different risks from working in different locations (home, public spaces)
- Equipment left in public view (in vehicles) should not be left unattended
- Consider insurance coverage
- Off-site backup should be stored at secure remote location
- Follow manufacturer instructions for prolonged storage

---

## 7.10 Storage Media
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Asset_management #Information_protection | #Protection #Defence

**Purpose:** Ensure only authorized disclosure, modification, removal, or destruction of information on storage media.

**Key Points:**
- Establish topic-specific policy for management of removable storage media
- Implement controls for management of removable media throughout their lifecycle
- Authorize media usage; record media in asset register; align with classification scheme
- Where encryption is used: ensure encryption strength is appropriate
- Return information on media to acceptable level before transfer
- Consider: need to use removable media; risk of using uncontrolled media; controls for media brought into organization
- Scan removable media received from outside for malware before use
- Maintain audit trail of removable media use
- Media stored to prevent unauthorized access per manufacturer guidelines
- Dispose of media no longer required securely: physically destroy; degauss; overwrite; use third party for disposal
- Maintain log of disposal of sensitive media

---

## 7.11 Supporting Utilities
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #Availability | #Protect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Prevent loss, damage, or compromise of information and other associated assets from failures in supporting utilities.

**Key Points:**
- Ensure equipment is protected from power failures and other disruptions
- Supporting utilities (electricity, water, sewage, heating/ventilation, air conditioning): adequate capacity to support systems
- Inspect and test supporting utilities regularly
- Alarms to detect malfunctions
- Emergency lighting and communications
- Provide multiple feeds with diverse physical routings for critical equipment
- UPS with sufficient power to enable orderly shutdown or continued operation
- Consider generator for extended power failures; regular testing of generators
- Ensure generator has adequate fuel supply; consider long-term fuel needs

---

## 7.12 Cabling Security
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #CIA | #Protect | #Physical_security #System_and_network_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Prevent loss, damage, or compromise of information and interruption to services from cable damage.

**Key Points:**
- Power and telecommunications cabling should be protected from interception or damage
- Power lines should be separated from communications cabling to prevent interference
- Use cable conduits or routes that avoid public areas where possible
- Network cabling should be protected; use sealed conduit or locked rooms or cabinets
- For sensitive or critical systems: consider armoured cabling, locked rooms/boxes at cable inspection points, use of electromagnetic shielding, regular inspection, random patching
- Consider fibre optic cabling
- Document cable routes and connections

---

## 7.13 Equipment Maintenance
**Attributes:** #Preventive #Corrective | #CIA | #Protect | #Physical_security | #Protection

**Purpose:** Prevent loss, damage, theft, or compromise of equipment and interruption to operations from inadequate maintenance.

**Key Points:**
- Carry out equipment maintenance per supplier recommendations and intervals
- Only authorized maintenance personnel should carry out repairs and service
- Keep records of all suspected or actual faults and all preventive and corrective maintenance
- Implement controls when sending equipment for maintenance off-site: sanitize sensitive information; insured; prevent access by uncontrolled maintenance personnel
- Comply with all maintenance requirements imposed by insurance policies
- Inspect equipment after maintenance to ensure no tampering and proper function
- Consider implications for re-certifying equipment after maintenance
- Consider risks of remotely managed equipment

---

## 7.14 Secure Disposal or Re-use of Equipment
**Attributes:** #Preventive | #Confidentiality | #Protect | #Physical_security #Information_protection | #Protection

**Purpose:** Prevent information leakage from equipment to be disposed of or re-used.

**Key Points:**
- Verify that any storage media containing sensitive information has been securely overwritten or destroyed before disposal
- All items of equipment containing storage media should be checked to ensure sensitive data and licensed software has been removed or securely overwritten prior to disposal or re-use
- Damaged storage devices: risk assess whether to destroy or repair
- Consider physical destruction for highly sensitive items
- Document destruction process
- For leased equipment: ensure information is removed prior to returning to the lessor
- Consider methods for secure disposal:
  - Physical destruction (shredding, crushing, melting)
  - Degaussing (for magnetic media)
  - Overwriting (using recognized techniques)
  - Cryptographic erasure (destroying encryption keys for encrypted data)
