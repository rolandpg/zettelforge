# ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — ISMS Requirements

**Edition:** Third edition, 2022-10  
**Replaces:** ISO/IEC 27001:2013 + Cor1:2014 + Cor2:2015  
**Key change:** Aligned with harmonized structure for management system standards and ISO/IEC 27002:2022

---

## Clause 4 — Context of the Organization

### 4.1 Understanding the Organization and Its Context
Determine external and internal issues relevant to ISMS purpose that affect ability to achieve intended outcomes.

### 4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties
Identify:
- Interested parties relevant to the ISMS
- Their relevant requirements
- Which requirements will be addressed through the ISMS

### 4.3 Determining the Scope of the ISMS
Determine ISMS boundaries and applicability (documented). Must consider:
- External/internal issues (4.1)
- Requirements of interested parties (4.2)
- Interfaces and dependencies between activities performed by the organization and those performed by other organizations

### 4.4 Information Security Management System
Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve an ISMS including processes needed and their interactions, in accordance with this standard.

## Clause 5 — Leadership

### 5.1 Leadership and Commitment
Top management must demonstrate leadership/commitment by:
1. Ensuring IS policy and objectives are established and compatible with strategic direction
2. Integration of ISMS into business processes
3. Ensuring resources are available
4. Communicating importance of effective IS management
5. Ensuring ISMS achieves intended outcomes
6. Directing and supporting persons to contribute
7. Promoting continual improvement
8. Supporting other management roles to demonstrate their leadership

### 5.2 Policy
Top management establishes an IS policy that:
- Is appropriate to the purpose of the organization
- Includes IS objectives or provides framework for setting them
- Includes commitment to satisfy applicable requirements
- Includes commitment to continual improvement of ISMS
- Is documented, communicated internally, available to interested parties as appropriate

### 5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities and Authorities
Top management assigns and communicates:
- Responsibility and authority to ensure ISMS conforms to standard requirements
- Responsibility and authority to report ISMS performance to top management

## Clause 6 — Planning

### 6.1.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities (General)
When planning ISMS, determine risks and opportunities that need to be addressed to:
- Ensure ISMS can achieve intended outcomes
- Prevent or reduce undesired effects
- Achieve continual improvement

Plan: actions to address risks/opportunities; how to integrate and implement actions; how to evaluate effectiveness.

### 6.1.2 Information Security Risk Assessment
Establish and apply an IS risk assessment process that:

**a) Establishes and maintains IS risk criteria including:**
- Risk acceptance criteria
- Criteria for performing IS risk assessments (to produce consistent, valid, comparable results)

**b) Identifies risks:**
- Apply risk assessment process to identify risks associated with loss of CIA for information in scope of ISMS
- Identify risk owners

**c) Analyses risks:**
- Assess potential consequences if risks materialize
- Assess realistic likelihood of occurrence
- Determine levels of risk

**d) Evaluates risks:**
- Compare results with risk criteria
- Prioritize analysed risks for treatment

**Retain documented information** about the IS risk assessment process.

### 6.1.3 Information Security Risk Treatment
Establish and apply an IS risk treatment process to:

**a)** Select appropriate treatment options (modify, retain, avoid, share)

**b)** Determine all controls necessary to implement selected treatment options

**c)** Compare controls with Annex A to verify no necessary controls have been omitted

**d)** Produce a Statement of Applicability (SoA) containing:
- Necessary controls (6.1.3 b and c)
- Justification for inclusion
- Whether they are implemented or not
- Justification for exclusion of controls from Annex A

**e)** Formulate an IS risk treatment plan

**f)** Obtain risk owners' approval of the IS risk treatment plan and acceptance of residual IS risks

**Retain documented information** about the IS risk treatment process.

> Note: The SoA is a critical audit artifact. It is the bridge between the risk assessment and the actual controls implemented.

### 6.2 Information Security Objectives and Planning to Achieve Them
IS objectives must be:
- Consistent with IS policy
- Measurable (if practicable)
- Take into account IS requirements and risk/treatment results
- Monitored
- Communicated
- Updated as appropriate
- **Documented**

When planning how to achieve IS objectives, determine:
- What will be done
- What resources will be required
- Who will be responsible
- When completed
- How results will be evaluated

### 6.3 Planning of Changes
Changes to the ISMS must be carried out in a planned manner.

## Clause 7 — Support

### 7.1 Resources
Determine and provide resources needed for establishment, implementation, maintenance and continual improvement of ISMS.

### 7.2 Competence
Determine necessary competence of persons doing work affecting IS performance. Ensure persons are competent. Take actions to acquire necessary competence. **Retain documented information** as evidence.

### 7.3 Awareness
Persons doing work under organization's control must be aware of:
- IS policy
- Their contribution to ISMS effectiveness including benefits of improved IS performance
- Implications of not conforming with ISMS requirements

### 7.4 Communication
Determine needs for internal and external communications relevant to ISMS including:
- What to communicate
- When to communicate
- With whom to communicate
- How to communicate

### 7.5 Documented Information

**7.5.1 General:** ISMS must include documented information required by this standard and determined by the organization as necessary for ISMS effectiveness.

**7.5.2 Creating and Updating:** When creating and updating documented information, ensure appropriate identification/description, format/media, review/approval for suitability/adequacy.

**7.5.3 Control of Documented Information:** Documented information required by ISMS must be controlled to ensure:
- Available and suitable for use, where and when needed
- Adequately protected (confidentiality, improper use, loss of integrity)
- Distribution, access, retrieval, use controlled
- Storage and preservation including preservation of legibility
- Control of changes (version control)
- Retention and disposition

Documented information of external origin determined necessary: identified and controlled.

## Clause 8 — Operation

### 8.1 Operational Planning and Control
Plan, implement, control, maintain, and review processes needed to meet IS requirements. Implement plans to achieve IS objectives. Control planned changes and review unintended changes; take action to mitigate adverse effects. Ensure externally provided processes, products, and services are controlled.

### 8.2 Information Security Risk Assessment
Perform IS risk assessments at planned intervals or when significant changes occur. **Retain documented information** of results.

### 8.3 Information Security Risk Treatment
Implement the IS risk treatment plan. **Retain documented information** of results.

---

## Clause 9 — Performance Evaluation

### 9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis and Evaluation
Determine:
- What needs to be monitored and measured (including IS processes and controls)
- Methods for monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation to ensure valid results
- When monitoring and measuring shall be performed
- Who shall monitor and measure
- When results shall be analysed and evaluated
- Who shall analyse and evaluate results

**Retain documented information** as evidence.

### 9.2 Internal Audit

**9.2.1 General:** Conduct internal audits at planned intervals to provide information on whether ISMS:
a) Conforms to organization's own requirements for ISMS and requirements of this standard
b) Is effectively implemented and maintained

**9.2.2 Internal Audit Programme:** Plan, establish, implement, maintain audit programme including frequency, methods, responsibilities, planning requirements, reporting. Take into consideration importance of processes concerned and results of previous audits. Define audit criteria and scope. Select objective and impartial auditors. **Retain documented information** as evidence.

### 9.3 Management Review

**9.3.1 General:** Top management reviews ISMS at planned intervals for continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness.

**9.3.2 Management Review Inputs:**
a) Status of actions from previous reviews
b) Changes in external/internal issues relevant to ISMS
c) Changes in needs/expectations of interested parties
d) Feedback on IS performance including trends in nonconformities/corrective actions, monitoring/measurement results, audit results, fulfilment of IS objectives
e) Feedback from interested parties
f) Results of risk assessment and status of risk treatment plan
g) Opportunities for continual improvement

**9.3.3 Management Review Results:** Include decisions related to continual improvement opportunities and any needs for changes to ISMS. **Retain documented information** as evidence.

---

## Clause 10 — Improvement

### 10.1 Continual Improvement
Continually improve suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the ISMS.

### 10.2 Nonconformity and Corrective Action
When nonconformity occurs:
a) React and control/correct it; deal with consequences
b) Evaluate need for action to eliminate causes (root cause)
c) Implement corrective actions
d) Review effectiveness of corrective actions
e) Make changes to ISMS if necessary

Corrective actions should be appropriate to effects of nonconformities.

**Retain documented information** of:
- Nature of nonconformities and subsequent actions
- Results of corrective actions
