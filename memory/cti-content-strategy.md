## Section 1: Content Pillars

Content pillars define the thematic boundaries. Each pillar has a defined purpose, target audience within the broader following, and a posting frequency.

### Pillar 1: Threat Intelligence & Alerts (25%)

**Purpose:** Demonstrate operational currency. Show the market Patrick sees things first and interprets them accurately.

**Content Types:**
- CVE/CISA KEV quick-take reactions (within hours of disclosure)
- Ransomware group activity summaries (Alphv/BlackCat, LockBit, Rhysida)

**Voice:** Clinical. "Here's what dropped, here's why it matters, here's what you should do." No hype. No fear-mongering.

**Target Audience:** MSSP operators, security practitioners, CISOs, threat researchers

**Example Hook:** *"CISA just added CVE-2024-XXXX to KEV. If you're running [affected product] in a DIB environment, this is your Tuesday problem. Here's the breakdown:"*

## Section 4: Voice & Format Guidelines

### Voice Principles

1. **Direct before diplomatic.** Patrick has opinions. Voice them with confidence and cite the reasoning. "The right answer is X because Y" beats "here are some perspectives."
2. **Short sentences.** Twitter rewards brevity. One idea per tweet. If a tweet needs a semicolon, split it.
3. **No corporate fluff.** No "leveraging synergistic solutions." No "at the end of the day." No "circle back."
4. **Dry humor earned.** A well-placed dry observation lands better than a joke. Avoid anything that tries too hard.
5. **Cite sources.** CISA, MITRE, vendor advisories, SEC filings, named companies. "I read somewhere" is not acceptable. "According to CISA's KEV catalog" is.
6. **Lead with the take.** Conclusion first. Supporting evidence second. Context last.
7. **Occasional contrarianism is healthy.** If conventional wisdom is wrong, say so and explain why. This is how thought leadership is built.

### Formatting Rules

- **No emoji in regular posts.** Exceptions: single emoji for emphasis in educational content or when it genuinely adds signal (not decoration).
- **Thread format:** Number each tweet (1/n, 2/n, etc.) so readers can track position.
- **Links:** Use short, relevant links. Never link to content behind a paywall without noting it.
- **Hashtags:** Use sparingly (1-2 max). Better to let the content speak for itself.
- **Images/GIFs:** Use when they genuinely add context. Not as a crutch for weak text.
- **Quote tweets:** Prefer adding value rather than just amplifying. "This, and here's why:" or "Disagree with this take:" beats silent retweets.

### Content That Does Not Get Posted

- Anything that could create liability for Summit 7 or Vigilance
- Client names, client situations, or anything that breaks confidentiality
- Hot takes on active incidents where Patrick doesn't have confirmed facts
- Political content of any kind
- Content attacking specific vendors unless it's a reasoned, documented critique (vendors can take criticism; people cannot)
- Anything posted while emotionally elevated

### Community Mapping

**Tier 2 — Engage when relevant:**
- PE/VC firms active in cybersecurity (Sentinel Capital, Crosspoint, BDT, etc.)
- Cybersecurity M&A commentators
- CMMC assessment ecosystem (CBOs, RPs)
- Conference organizers and cybersecurity event accounts

**Tier 3 — Monitor for intelligence:**
- Dark web monitoring accounts (Flashpoint, Recorded Future, Intel 471)
- Ransomware group leak sites (for context, never amplify)
- Industry news accounts (BleepingComputer, Krebs, DarkReading, The Hacker News)

### Engagement Rules

1. **Reply within 2 hours during business hours.** X's algorithm rewards engagement velocity. The faster Patrick responds to relevant conversations, the more visibility the account gets.
2. **Add value in replies.** A reply should never just be "agreed." Expand on the point, add a counterpoint, cite additional data.
3. **Ask questions back.** Conversation drives follower growth. "Have you seen this in your environment?" converts lurkers into participants.
4. **Engage with PE/VC content.** Comment on cybersecurity M&A news, PE platform buildouts, MSSP acquisitions. This puts Patrick in the feeds of the audience he wants to reach.
5. **Amplify peers.** MSSP operators, security practitioners, DIB commentators. Never engage negatively with competitors publicly.

### Growth Tactics

1. **Conference live-tweeting.** RSA, DEF CON, S4, DHS CISA events, CMMC events. Live commentary generates followers who were physically present and are searching for coverage.
2. **Quote tweet with add.** When sharing industry news, always add Patrick's take rather than a silent retweet. This positions him as an interpreter, not a repeater.
3. **Engagement pods (use cautiously).** The Twitter Engager framework suggests community engagement groups. Use only for genuine interaction — algorithmicPods that just boost engagement metrics hurt more than help.
4. **Cross-platform signals.** LinkedIn posts that reference X content drive cross-platform traffic. Reference the other platform naturally: "I posted a thread on this earlier — link in bio."

## Section 9: Operational Notes

### CTI Pipeline Integration

The existing 6 AM automated briefing pipeline (CVE + CSAF advisories) is the anchor of the content operation. It should:
1. Auto-post to X at 6:00 AM CST without manual intervention
2. Include a brief, Patrick-written header (not just raw output) summarizing the most critical item
3. Link back to relevant CISA pages for each item
4. Be reviewed by Patrick before posting (or a trusted delegate) if the system supports human-in-the-loop

**Recommended enhancement:** Flag items with DIB relevance (DoD contractors, defense suppliers, CUI-adjacent) in the daily brief with a [DIB] tag. This allows Patrick to expand on those items in the 8 AM post.

### Cross-Platform Adaptation

| X Content | LinkedIn Adaptation |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Threat alert threads | Expand to short-form blog post; executive summary for non-practitioners |
| MSSP operations insights | LinkedIn article; more business framing (margins, growth, PE relevance) |
| DIB/CMMC commentary | LinkedIn article with policy depth |

| BTS/automation | LinkedIn video or demo reel if tooling permits |

LinkedIn posts should go up within 24-48 hours of the X thread. Reference X: "I posted a thread on this topic earlier this week — here's the expanded version."

### What Not to Automate

- Personal/philosophical content
- Industry commentary with opinions
- Engagement replies (these must be human)
- Any content referencing Summit 7 clients or specific engagements

### Performance Review Cadence

- **Weekly:** Review X Analytics for engagement rate, top-performing posts, follower growth

- **Quarterly:** Full strategic review against goals; adjust schedule if needed
